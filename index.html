Types of Appeals Specialized by Brownstone Law

1. Criminal Appeals

Criminal appeals are one of the primary areas of focus for Brownstone Law. These appeals arise when a defendant seeks to challenge a conviction or sentence imposed by a lower court. The firm handles various aspects of criminal appeals, including:

  • Direct Appeals: This involves appealing a conviction directly to a higher court, typically based on legal errors that occurred during the trial.
  • Post-Conviction Relief: Clients may seek to overturn their convictions or reduce their sentences through post-conviction motions, such as motions for a new trial or habeas corpus petitions.
  • Sentencing Appeals: Brownstone Law assists clients in appealing sentences that they believe are excessively harsh or unjust, advocating for fair treatment under the law.

The firm’s extensive experience in criminal appeals ensures that clients receive knowledgeable representation throughout the process.

2. Civil Appeals

In addition to criminal appeals, Brownstone Law also specializes in civil appeals. These appeals can arise from various civil matters, including:

  • Contract Disputes: Clients can appeal decisions related to contract breaches or interpretations that they believe were wrongly adjudicated.
  • Personal Injury Cases: If a client believes that a verdict in a personal injury case was unjust, they can appeal the decision to a higher court.
  • Business Litigation: Brownstone Law represents businesses in appealing decisions that may affect their operations, such as partnership disputes or employment law issues.

The firm’s expertise in civil appeals allows clients to challenge unfavorable rulings effectively and seek justice.

3. Family Law Appeals

xFamily law cases often involve emotionally charged issues, and Brownstone Law provides specialized representation in family law appeals. This includes:

  • Divorce Settlements: Clients seeking to appeal divorce settlements that they believe are inequitable can rely on Brownstone Law for expert guidance.
  • Child Custody and Support: The firm assists clients in appealing decisions regarding child custody arrangements and support orders, advocating for their rights as parents.
  • Adoption Cases: Brownstone Law represents clients in appealing decisions related to adoption, ensuring that their interests are protected throughout the process.

By focusing on family law appeals, Brownstone Law helps clients navigate the complexities of family-related legal matters.

4. Federal Appeals

Brownstone Law has a strong emphasis on federal appeals, representing clients in various federal court cases. This includes:

  • Habeas Corpus Petitions: The firm assists clients in filing habeas corpus petitions to challenge the legality of their detention under federal law.
  • Federal Civil Rights Appeals: Clients can appeal decisions involving violations of their civil rights, ensuring that justice is served.
  • Federal Criminal Appeals: Brownstone Law represents clients in appealing federal criminal convictions, addressing issues such as prosecutorial misconduct or ineffective assistance of counsel.

The firm’s expertise in federal appeals ensures that clients receive competent representation in complex legal matters.

5. Administrative Appeals

In addition to traditional court appeals, Brownstone Law also specializes in administrative appeals. These appeals often involve challenging decisions made by government agencies, such as:

  • Licensing and Regulatory Matters: Clients can appeal decisions related to professional licenses or regulatory compliance that they believe are unjust.
  • Social Security Appeals: The firm assists clients in appealing denials of Social Security benefits, advocating for their rights to receive the support they need.
  • Immigration Appeals: Brownstone Law represents clients in appealing immigration decisions, ensuring that their cases are handled with care and expertise.

By offering administrative appeal services, Brownstone Law provides comprehensive support for clients facing challenges with government agencies.
